Problem · scattered media

Finding a photo is easy. Knowing whether it is safe is hard.

Media lives across Drive folders, Google Photos, ResourceSpace records, and local batches. The missing layer is clear reuse permission.

Say: “The question is not only ‘where is the file?’ It is ‘can this be used publicly, internally, or not at all?’”

Current frictionFolder guessing

Users need to know where media was stored before they can search it.

RiskUnclear approval

Approved-looking media can still lack rights, source, people, or youth evidence.

TargetGuided reuse

Every asset should answer what use is allowed before download.

Portal model · plain explanation

ResourceSpace stays source of truth. The portal becomes the safe front door.

Google Shared Drive holds originals. ResourceSpace tracks records and metadata. The new portal turns that into a role-aware ministry workflow.

Say: “We are not replacing ResourceSpace or Drive. We are making them usable for normal ministry workers.”

User journey · nontechnical

Four roles. One clear path.

Users find media, contributors send media, reviewers approve media, admins manage readiness.

ViewerFind and download approved copies.

Viewer sees safe labels, usage guidance, and request-review paths.

ContributorSend files or source links.

Every intake starts Needs Review / Do Not Publish.

ReviewerApprove only with evidence.

Rights, people/youth, source, reviewer, date, and notes stay explicit.

Say: “Many people can submit media. Few people should approve media. Very few people should change system settings.”

Review + governance

The pilot is intentionally honest about what is still blocked.

Reviewer actions queue pending writes until ResourceSpace write mapping is configured. Governance shows launch blockers instead of hiding them.

Say: “This prevents the demo from pretending final approval happened when the backend is not yet wired for production writes.”

Q&A guardrails

Keep answers simple and safety-centered.

Stakeholders need confidence in the workflow, not backend details.

Close: “This portal is not just a prettier photo library. It is a safety layer for ministry media reuse.”

Is this replacing Google Drive?

No. Google Shared Drive remains the master warehouse for originals.

Is this replacing ResourceSpace?

No. ResourceSpace remains the source of truth. The portal is the safer ministry-facing layer.

Can normal users upload?

Yes, but upload only submits for review. It does not publish.

Can normal users download everything?

No. Viewer downloads stay blocked unless the asset is portal-ready.

What about children or youth photos?

They remain review-required unless explicit approval evidence exists.

Is this production-ready?

No. It is a pilot demo candidate. Production still needs auth, deployment, backups, write mapping, reviewer assignment, and launch checks.
